Voice actors

a person whose job is to provide the voices for characters in animated films, dubbed foreign language films, audio dramas,animes, etc..

famous japanese voice actors

  • Yūki Kajis


    • Eren Jaeger's signature scream is the work of prolific voice actor Yūki Kaji, who has worked in the industry since 2004. Besides providing the voices for a wide variety of anime characters, Kaji also hosts a web radio series and variety show. In addition, he once participated in a four-person singing group.

  • Hiroshi Kamiya


    • Hiroshi Kamiya is a Japanese voice actor from Chiba, Japan, who's been active in the industry since 1994. In addition to playing the protagonists of a variety of series, he also hosts a radio show and provides the voices for Boys Love CDs - audio stories from the BL genre.

  • Mamoru Miyano


    • If you remember Light Yagami taking a potato chip and eating it, or the character Rin Matsuoka crying, you remember Mamoru Miyano's voice. Miyano began voice acting in 1990 and, in 2007, he began working as a pop and rock musician. He has won multiple awards, including "best musical performance" in the sixth and eighth Seiyuu awards, as well as "best lead actor" in the 2011 Newtype Awards.

  • Daisuke Ono


    • Daisuke Ono is an established voice acting talent who is responsible for a number of protagonists' voices. In an impressive victory, Ono won the fourth and ninth Seiyu Awards for "best lead actor" for his roles as Sebastian Michaelis and Jotaro Kujo. He also hosts a radio show called Dear Girls Stories along with fellow voice actor Hiroshi Kamiya.

  • Romi Park


    • Romi Park is a Korean-Japanese voice actress who was born in Edogawa, Tokyo. She tends to play tough, aggressive female characters and male characters who are considered geniuses - Edward Elric (one of her most notable roles) is a clear example of this. Park has also provided voices for video game characters and Japanese dubs of Western media, such as Guardians of the Galaxy and The Matrix Revolutions.

  • Jun Fukuyama


    • Jun Fukuyama has a wide range of vocal abilities, lending his talents to unique characters like Lelouch and Koro-sensei. In addition to his anime work, he sometimes lends his voice to Japanese dubs of Western media like Game of Thrones and Harry Potter. In 2017, he announced his intention to host a concert, proving himself to be a musical talent as well.

  • Miyuki Sawashiro


    • Miyuki Sawashiro is an acclaimed voice actor who won the Special Jury Prize for her very first role in Di Gi Charat. Sawashiro is one of the only voice actors who's played the same role in both the Japanese and English versions of a show (Puchiko in Leave it to Piyoko). She even moved abroad to Pennsylvania to learn and practice English.

  • Rie Kugimiya


    • Rie Kugimiya is an award-winning voice actress active in the industry since 1992. Because she's lent her voice to several tsundere characters like Taiga Aisaka and Louise, she's known to some as the "queen of tsundere."

  • Kana Hanazawa


    • Kana Hanazawa is a multi-talented actress involved in voice acting, singing, and live-action performances. She began her voice acting career while still a college student, playing the lead role in the anime Kobato. In 2015, she won the award for Best Supporting Actress in the 9th Seiyu Awards, as well as Best Actress in the Newtype Anime Awards.

  • Tatsuhisa Suzuki


    • Tatsuhisa Suzuki has worked as a voice actor since 2002. He's represented by both I'm Enterprise and Lantis. In 2002, he started singing for the band OLDCODEX under the stage name Ta_2. OLDCODEX has provided the theme songs for several anime, including Free! and Servamp.
